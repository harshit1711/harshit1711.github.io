How Overlap-Based Scheduling Works

  • Convert candidate and interviewer working hours into a common baseline.
  • Compute overlapping windows only.
  • Prioritize slots near each person’s core working hours (avoid early/late extremes).
  • Account for daylight saving changes automatically.
Best practice: offer 2-3 slot options from the overlap window instead of one slot. This reduces rescheduling churn significantly.

Practical Scheduling Rules

  • Avoid candidate slots before 8:00 AM local time unless explicitly requested.
  • Avoid interviewer slots after 7:00 PM local time for first-round interviews.
  • For panel rounds, prioritize shared overlap with highest cumulative convenience.
  • For global teams, use fixed "interview bands" to simplify coordination.
For cross-continent scheduling, pick overlap first, then optimize for role seniority and interviewer availability.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Scheduling in UTC without confirming local daytime context.
  • Ignoring DST shifts when using copied old schedules.
  • Assuming the same overlap works for technical, managerial, and panel rounds.
  • Sending only one slot option for international interviews.
